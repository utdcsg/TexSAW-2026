
UNIVERSAL RULES
All ranged attacks are always considered within normal range even when in melee range.

Read attacks and spells, they have been changed from base D&D 5e.

Additional attacks/spell effects are not rolled for if intended target is dead. Full damage however, is still rolled.

When rolling multiple attacks it always goes attack, damage, attack, damage, etc.

Only when a behavior says it moves out of melee range does a creature get an opportunity attack, moving into another creature’s melee range does not provoke an opportunity attack.

When landing a critical hit, roll twice the number of damage dice


DM
When initiative is rolled, Players roll in order of priority (low to high) then NPCs
In the case of multiple creatures of the same type, each is assigned a number in increasing order. The NPCs will take their turn on their initiative in order of lowest to highest.
When calling for a 1 player skill check roll 1d20, the first one determines the target, if its (all following numbers are inclusive) between 2-7 they check is for the player with priority 1, if its between 8-13 the check is for the player with priority 2, if its between 14-19 the check is for the player with the priority 3. On a 1 or a 20 reroll this die.
All times damage is rounded it is rounded up.	
When checks are called for the whole party they are made in order of increasing priority.
Damage caused by a failed skill check is rolled immediately before rolling the check for the next party member.
In the event initiative and Dex scores are tied, players go first in initiative order.


Players
At the start of their turn, each player will roll 1d20 to decide their behavior this turn
During combat, players will attack the creature with the priority from lowest to highest number.

Players will always make an opportunity attack if possible.
Remember to add the appropriate ability modifier to the damage of all weapons (NOT SPELLS)

Once a player reaches 0hp, they make death saves on their turn, of they reach 3 successes they are restored to 1 hit point on their turn. If they fail 3 death saves they are dead and do not participate in the campaign.

NPCs
At the start of an NPCs turn, they roll 2d20, the first one determines their target, if its (all following numbers are inclusive) between 2-7 they attack the player with priority 1, if its between 8-13 they attack the player with priority 2, if its between 14-19 they attack the player with the priority 3. On a 1 or a 20 or if rolling the priority of a dead player, they reroll this die before rolling the second die. The second die they roll on their behavior chart.


NPCs will always make an opportunity attack if possible

NPCs do not get death saves

Ability score modifiers to damage are included in the attack descriptions if applicable
